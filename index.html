Blacklist Domains

Block all the things!

Hi, I'm WaLLy3K (a moderator over at /r/pihole) and I've compiled this list to help people have a more enjoyable online presence - with less ads, trackers and malicious content. These blocklists have original content (thanks to the painstaking work of each list maintainer), and of course, are compatible with Pi-hole. These lists may work with other adblocking tools, but YMMV!

Before starting, here's some cliffnotes:
  • The changelog for this page is found here on GitHub
  • If you're looking for my other project, you can find 'Pi-hole Block Page' here
  • Lists bulleted with a tick are suggested for their minimal false positives
  • Lists bulleted with a cross are known for higher than average false positives, or has blocked "popular" sites (e.g: Amazon AWS, Google, Netflix)
  • These lists are installed to /etc/pihole/adlists.list, which needs to be created if it doesn't already exist
  • The formatting for adlists.list is the same as adlists.default: Customising Sources for Ad Lists
  • Some of these lists are mirrored by myself, due to the source not being a 'domains only' list. If this (or the lack of TLS) concerns you, then consider parsing them yourself
Installing too many lists can BREAK STUFF. Here's how you can fix that:

Blocking over 1 million domains can result in the Query Log tool of Pi-hole to return an ajax datatables error, when it loads. The recommended way to work around this is to avoid certain lists known for being extremely overzealous, such as hosts.eladkarako.com. If you're a masochist and have hardware with 1GB of RAM or more, you can increase PHP's memory by changing memory_limit to read 180M within its config. That's found at /etc/php5/fpm/php.ini OR /etc/php5/cgi/php.ini, and then made active by sudo service php5-fpm restart OR sudo service php5-cgi restart. Raising PHP's memory beyond this suggestion may result in your hardware becoming unresponsive!

  • webcache.googleusercontent.com Google Cache (Elad Karako)
  • clients2.google.com Google apps connectivity (Andy Short)
  • clients3.google.com Google apps connectivity (Andy Short)
  • clients4.google.com Google apps connectivity (Andy Short)
  • clients5.google.com Google apps connectivity (Andy Short)
